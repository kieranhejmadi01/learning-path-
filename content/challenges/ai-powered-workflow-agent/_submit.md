Congratulations on building your solution, we are excited to see what you have created and appreciate the effort you’ve put into preparing your submission. Please follow the steps below to share your solution with us. 

### Step 1. Prepare your repository

If you are sharing your solution through a public GitHub repository, make sure reviewers can understand and validate your work.

- Include clear steps to reproduce your results.
- Preferably include a demonstration of your solution, for example with a video. 
- Add a `README.md` file that describes your solution, setup, and how to run it.

### Step 2. Submit a Pull Request

To submit your work, open a pull request against the [Arm Learning Path repository](https://github.com/ArmDeveloperEcosystem/arm-learning-paths) and update the challenge `_index.md` file for this challenge with your submission details.

Add your entry under `community_attempts` using this pattern:

```yaml
community_attempts:
  - link: ""
    title: ""
    author: ""
    summary: ""
```

- Use the same quoted string format for `link`, `title`, `author`, and `summary`.
- Add the project link, title, author name, and a short summary.
- Keep the summary under 30 words.

Finally, raise the pull request template and confirm that your contribution does not include confidential or proprietary information, as described in the pull request template.

### Hearing back from us

We sincerely appreciate the time and effort you invested and hope you gained valuable experience along the way. Submissions are assessed against the criteria in the `Challenge brief` section, and the stand out solutions will be featured on on the main page of the challenge. As a token of recognition, some participants may still receive a digital badge for their efforts, which can be shared on platforms such as LinkedIn.

If you enjoyed this challenge, we encourage you to explore opportunities with us—Arm is actively looking for individuals with skills like the ones you have developed. Visit  [our career page](https://careers.arm.com/) to learn more.
